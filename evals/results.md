**Question:** Is the inductance setting on the OmniPro's LCD useful for short-circuit transfer on 0.030 solid wire, or is it better left at the default?

> Good question — let me pull the exact inductance behavior from the manual before answering.Short answer: yes, inductance is genuinely useful for short-circuit transfer on 0.030 solid wire — that's exactly the transfer mode where it earns its keep.
> 
> **What the manual says (page 21):** Inductance adjusts arc length. *Increase* for a more fluid puddle and flatter bead; *decrease* for a colder puddle.
> 
> **What's actually happening electrically:** In short-circuit transfer, the wire dips into the puddle ~40–200 times/sec, shorting and clearing. Inductance controls how fast current ramps up after each short clears. 
> 
> - **Higher inductance** → slower current rise → softer arc re-ignition, less spatter, wetter/flatter bead, but a slightly "lazy" arc that can stub if WFS is too low.
> - **Lower inductance** → faster current rise → crisper, snappier arc with more "crackle," stiffer puddle, easier out-of-position starts, but more spatter and a more crowned bead.
> 
> **For 0.030 solid wire specifically:**
> - **Carbon steel, flat/horizontal, C25 gas** → nudge inductance **up** from default. You'll hear the arc soften from a harsh crackle to a smoother sizzle, and spatter drops noticeably.
> - **Thin sheet (18–22 ga), tacking, or vertical-up** → leave at default or go slightly **down**. You want a colder, faster-freezing puddle so you don't blow through or sag.
> - **Out-of-position or starts on cold plate** → lower inductance helps the arc bite in and stay lit.
> 
> The default is a reasonable middle ground, so don't touch it until you've dialed in WFS and voltage first. Inductance is a *finishing* adjustment — tune it by ear and by bead appearance after the base parameters are right. Per page 21, adjust, stop, inspect, repeat.
> 
> One caveat: the OmniPro exposes inductance as a relative setting, not a Henry value, so "increase 2 clicks" is the right mental model, not "set it to 40%."
